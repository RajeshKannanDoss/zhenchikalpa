Fever

Fever is one of the most common reasons people seek healthcare. While it can be uncomfortable, fever is often regarded as part of the body’s response to illness.

What Is Fever?

Fever refers to an elevation in body temperature above the normal range. It may occur in response to infections, inflammatory conditions, or other physiological processes.

Common Symptoms

  • Increased body temperature
  • Chills
  • Fatigue
  • Sweating
  • Body aches
  • Reduced appetite

Common Causes

  • Viral infections
  • Bacterial infections
  • Inflammatory conditions
  • Reactions to medications
  • Other medical conditions

Individualized Assessment

Every person experiences illness differently. At Zhenchi Kalpa Acupuncture Clinic, each patient is assessed individually, taking into account symptoms, overall health, and personal circumstances.

Supportive Care

General supportive measures may include:

  • Adequate hydration
  • Rest
  • Appropriate nutrition
  • Monitoring symptoms
  • Seeking medical assessment when necessary

Frequently Asked Questions

Is fever always harmful?

Not necessarily. Fever is commonly regarded as one of the body’s responses during illness. However, persistent or severe symptoms should be assessed by a qualified healthcare professional.

When should I seek urgent medical attention?

Seek immediate medical care if symptoms are severe, worsening, or accompanied by concerning signs such as difficulty breathing, confusion, or other urgent symptoms.
