Fever: A Healing Response and the Role of Acupuncture

Few symptoms create as much anxiety as fever. The moment the thermometer shows a higher-than-normal temperature, many people immediately look for ways to bring it down. However, fever itself is not a disease. It is one of the body’s natural responses to illness and infection.

Throughout my clinical practice, I have observed that many patients fear fever more than the underlying condition. Yet, from a holistic perspective, fever often represents the body’s effort to restore balance and defend itself.

What Is Fever?

Fever is a temporary rise in body temperature that usually occurs when the body is responding to an infection or other challenge. It is a sign that the immune system has become active.

Many people experience symptoms such as:

  • Increased body temperature
  • Chills
  • Sweating
  • Fatigue
  • Body aches
  • Reduced appetite
  • General weakness

Looking Beyond the Temperature

In acupuncture and traditional healing systems, practitioners do not focus only on the number shown on a thermometer. Instead, the individual is assessed as a whole.

Final Thoughts

At Zhenchi Kalpa Acupuncture Clinic, we believe in treating the individual, not merely the symptom.
